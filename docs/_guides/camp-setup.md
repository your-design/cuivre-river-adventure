## Arrival: Clean and Inspect Like an Expert

Before you drop a single jack or stake, do what seasoned overlanders do—**read the
site, clean it, and make it yours.** Fifteen minutes here prevents days of ants,
puddles, and "why is our tent on a slope?"

### 1. Walk and inspect first
- **Look up:** any dead limbs or hanging branches over a tent/RV spot ("widowmakers")? Don't park under them.
- **Look down:** slope and drainage—find the **high, flat ground**; note where rain would run.
- **Scan for hazards:** fire-ant mounds, ground-wasp/bee holes, poison ivy ("leaves of three"),
  broken glass, old tent stakes, and animal burrows.
- **Check the fixtures:** fire ring condition, picnic table, the electric pedestal
  breaker, water spigot, and how far to the bathhouse.

### 2. Sweep away the welcome mat
- **Sweep the pad and tent/galley footprints** with a stiff broom. This clears crumbs
  and—per the overlanders—**wipes out the ant scout scent-trails** that lead colonies
  straight to your kitchen. A clean pad is your first and best pest defense.
- Knock down spider webs under the table and around the pedestal.

### 3. Treat the perimeter—gently
- Dust a **light perimeter of food-grade diatomaceous earth (DE)** around tent edges,
  RV stabilizer jacks, the pod's feet, and the power-cord "bridge" to the ground.
  Add a little **cinnamon or peppermint** near the galleys. **No harsh ground
  insecticides**—they're discouraged/prohibited in parks and toxic to kids, dogs, and
  the creek. (Full method: [Savvy Camper → eco pest control](/guides/savvy-camper#eco-friendly-pest-control).)

### 4. Then set up
Now level, stake, and unroll—on ground you've actually chosen.

## Positioning the Two Sites

With two sites holding a big RV, a pod, a tent, four vehicles, awnings, and a group
kitchen, **placement matters.** A rough plan (adjust to the real sites):

**Site 1 — large RV + 2 cars**
- Park the RV **level**, with the **awning/outdoor kitchen opening toward the shared
  gathering area** and toward **afternoon shade**.
- Keep **slide-outs and the awning clear** of trees, posts, and the neighbor's space.
- Park the **two cars tandem or to one side** so they don't block the path or the awning;
  use one as a **windbreak** for the kitchen if it's breezy.
- Set the **dogs' shaded tie-out/x-pen** on the quiet side of the RV.

**Site 2 — pod + tent + 2 cars**
- Put the **pod on the firmest, most level pad** (trailers hate slopes).
- Pitch the **big tent on the highest, flattest, best-draining spot**, away from where
  water would run, ideally with **morning sun / afternoon shade**.
- Stage the **group HQ canopy + big tables** between the two sites (or on the roomier
  pad) so meals are central and the **kid corridor between camps stays open and visible**.
- Park cars to **buffer wind and the road**, not to wall off the campfire.

**For both:** keep the **campfire downwind of tents and awnings**, gear paths clear, and
the **kid zone visible from the camp chairs.**

{: .note }
> **One camp, not two.** Pick the roomier site as "Group HQ" for the canopy, big table,
> group kitchen, water jug, and daily [coffee station](/guides/camp-jobs#the-coffee-station).

## Setup Order

Aim to **arrive early-to-mid afternoon** for daylight and energy.

1. **Inspect & clean** each pad (above).
2. **Level & hook up** the RV and pod; **pitch the tent** on the spot you chose.
3. **Raise Group HQ** — canopy, tables, water jug, group kitchen, coffee station.
4. **Safety walk with the kids** — bathrooms, boundaries, the fire ring ("hot circle"),
   the dogs' area, and "no water without an adult."
5. **Beds before dark** — make beds, stage headlamps and the **box fans**.
6. **Nice-to-have** — string lights, rugs, hammocks, kiddie pool, fire prep.

## Beating the July Heat

July in Missouri is hot and humid—plan the day around it.

- **Schedule around midday.** Active stuff early and late; rest/shade/water **1–3:30**.
- **Box fans are a must.** On the electric RV/pod sites, a box fan transforms naps and
  nights. Bring one per sleeping space if you can.
- **Set up a kiddie pool** in the shade—gold for the little ones (and the dogs) to cool
  off at camp. **Dump it daily** (mosquitoes breed in standing water) and keep soap out of it.
- **Hydrate constantly**—refill the group jug; electrolyte packets on big-activity days.
- **Shade is everything:** canopies, the tree line, the A/C pod, and the creek.
- **Cool off** at the [swim beach](/activities/swimming) or by wading Big Sugar Creek.

## Dogs at Camp

The Older Smiths are bringing **two dogs**, so a little planning keeps everyone happy:

- **Leashed (10-ft max) and attended at all times**—park rule, and right next to other
  families and wildlife. Never leave dogs tied and unattended at the site.
- Set a **shaded tie-out or x-pen** by the RV with **water always available**; the
  **kiddie pool** doubles as a dog cool-down.
- **Watch the heat**—dogs overheat fast; midday is for shade and water, not the trail.
- **Pick up after them** every time; bag it and pack it out.
- **Check dogs for ticks** nightly along with the kids.
- Know **where dogs are allowed**—they're fine on trails and around camp, but **not on
  the swim beach**. Confirm specifics with the office.
- Bring **vaccination records** and keep tags on.

## Thunderstorms

Missouri summer afternoons can turn stormy fast.

- **Watch the sky and a weather app** in the afternoon.
- **Secure or drop the canopy** before wind hits—a loose canopy becomes a sail. Stow the RV awning.
- **Off the water at the first rumble.** Lightning means everyone out of the lake/creek.
- **Stage a rain plan:** the RV and pod are dry shelters; pull out cards and the
  [kids' printables](/kids). Most storms pass within an hour. (A rainy day is also a
  good cue to run the [supply run](/itinerary/day3) or a [day trip](/day-trips/).)

## Bugs & Plants

- **Ticks & chiggers:** repellent on skin, **permethrin on clothes/shoes**, tuck pants
  into socks on trails, and a **tick check every night** (kids and dogs especially).
- **Ants:** the swept pad + DE perimeter (above) is your defense—plus sealed food.
- **Mosquitoes:** worst at dusk—long sleeves, repellent, a smoky fire, and **no standing
  water** (dump the kiddie pool).
- **Poison ivy:** "leaves of three, let it be." Show the kids on day one.
- **Fireflies:** the good bug—evening firefly-catching is a highlight (let them go after).

Full eco-friendly method: [Savvy Camper & RV Tips](/guides/savvy-camper#eco-friendly-pest-control).

## Fire & Cooking Safety

- Fires **only in the provided ring/grill.** Check for posted **burn bans** at the office.
- Keep a **water bucket** at the fire; **drown, stir, and feel** before bed/leaving.
- Kids stay an arm's reach back; teach the "hot circle" around the ring.
- Store food sealed in coolers/bins—raccoons and ringtails are clever overnight.
- Mind **butane/propane** safety: cook outside, never inside a tent or the pod.

## Camp Hygiene & Kids

- **Handwashing station:** a jug with a spigot + soap by the kitchen; sanitizer everywhere.
- **Bathroom buddy system** after dark; everyone has a headlamp.
- **Wet-stuff line:** a clothesline for swimsuits and towels (you'll have many).
- **A bin per kid** for their own gear keeps two sites from becoming one big lost-and-found.

## A Few House Rules

Not to squash anyone's fun—these are just the handful of things that keep a 12-person
camp clean, cool, and bug-free all week. (There's a kid-friendly version on the
[Kids at Camp](/kids#our-camp-rules-we-all-help) page.)

- **No fireworks or firecrackers.** The campground prohibits them—**please don't bring
  any.** We'll caravan to the [Troy Rotary show](/itinerary/day4) instead, with glow
  sticks for the kids.
- **Paper plates, bowls, and plastic utensils.** The **Older Smiths are bringing plenty
  for everyone.** It feels wasteful, but with a group this size it's the fastest way to
  keep the site clean and the dishwater down.
- **Eat and drink *outside* the RVs—water is the only exception.** No food or other
  drinks inside. If you **open a drink, finish it and toss the container**—don't leave
  it sitting. And **no in-and-out, in-and-out** through the RV doors: every open door
  lets bugs in and the A/C out. In or out, then keep it shut.
- **Refill the filtered-water pitcher when you fill a bottle from it.** Top it back off
  for the next person.
- **Don't cut through other families' campsites**—or the neighbors'. Use the paths and
  the open kid corridor between our two sites.
- **Hang up your wet things.** Swimsuits, towels, **and your bath/shower kit** go on the
  wet-stuff line—not dumped on a bed or an outdoor table.

## Quiet Hours & Good-Neighbor Camp

- **Quiet hours 10 p.m.–6 a.m.**—wind down the volume, not necessarily the fun.
- Keep generators (if any) within posted hours.
- Leashed, attended pets only.
- **Leave No Trace:** pack out trash, keep the site cleaner than you found it.
