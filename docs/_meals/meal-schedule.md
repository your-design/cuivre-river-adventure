## How to Read This

- **Dinner** = group meal, with a **host family** who cooks and leads cleanup.
- **Breakfast & lunch** = per-family / grab-and-go. The options listed are the shared
  pantry everyone pulls from—make what your crew wants.
- ⭐ = a make-ahead item; prep it before the trip ([Meal Prep guide](/meals/meal-prep)).

## Day-by-Day

### Wednesday, July 1 — Arrival 🌭
*Travel day. Keep it easy.*

- **Dinner — Hot Dogs & Brats Cookout** *(host: Zuelke)*
  - Hot dogs + **brats**, buns, ketchup/mustard/relish
  - **Variety-pack chips**, **watermelon**, baby carrots
  - Easy after setup; grill is fast and the kids are happy
- **Snacks on the road:** chips, fruit, string cheese, applesauce pouches, water

---

### Thursday, July 2 — Settle In
- **Breakfast — Big Camp Breakfast:** scrambled **eggs**, **bacon** ⭐, **hashbrowns**;
  cereal/yogurt/bananas for the kids; coffee
- **Lunch — Sandwich Bar:** **PB&J** (grape & strawberry), turkey & ham sandwiches,
  string cheese, chips, grapes & apples, veggies
- **Dinner — Burgers & Dogs** *(host: Harris)*
  - **Hamburgers** ⭐ (patties pre-made) + hot dogs
  - **Sweet corn on the cob**, chips, cold veggie tray & dip

---

### Friday, July 3 — Taco Night
- **Breakfast — Quick & Cold:** cereal (fruity pebbles, honeynut cheerios), **vanilla
  yogurt cups**, **cinnamon-swirl bagels** with strawberry cream cheese, fruit; coffee
- **Lunch — Grilled Cheese** + tomato soup; baked Lays, goldfish, applesauce
- **Dinner — Taco Bar / Walking Tacos** *(host: Younger Smiths)*
  - ⭐ **Pre-cooked seasoned meat** (Mexican-style chicken/pork/steak), reheated
  - **Rice**, warm **tortillas**, shredded cheese, lettuce, salsa, sour cream
  - **Walking-taco option:** crush a single-serve **Fritos/Doritos** bag, spoon in meat
    + toppings—huge kid hit, minimal dishes

---

### Saturday, July 4 — Independence Day 🎆
*The big feast. Everyone contributes; Older Smiths coordinate the main.*

- **Breakfast — Festive Pancakes & Bacon** ⭐ (red-white-blue with berries if you like);
  eggs for anyone who wants them; coffee
- **Lunch — Picnic Sandwiches & Chips:** keep it light—there's a feast tonight
- **Dinner — 🎆 BBQ FEAST** *(all families; Older Smiths lead)*
  - ⭐ **Pulled pork** (cooked & frozen ahead, reheated) + **BBQ chicken thighs**
  - **Baked beans**, **mac & cheese**, **sweet corn on the cob**
  - **Watermelon**, garden salad, rolls/bread
  - **Dessert:** s'mores + a red-white-blue treat (berry trifle or flag cake)
- **Evening:** campfire, naturalist program if offered. *Note: check the park's rules on
  fireworks—personal fireworks are typically **not allowed** in state parks; plan on
  glow sticks and a great campfire instead.*

---

### Sunday, July 5 — Easy Day
- **Breakfast — Biscuits & Gravy** ⭐ + scrambled eggs; fruit; coffee
- **Lunch — PB&J + Leftovers** (Fourth-of-July encore: pulled-pork sandwiches!)
- **Dinner — Spaghetti & Salad** *(host: Older Smiths)*
  - ⭐ **Spaghetti** with meat sauce (sauce made ahead), parmesan

---

### Monday, July 6 — Use-It-Up Grill Night
- **Breakfast — Cinnamon Rolls** (Pillsbury) + fruit; cereal; coffee

  - **Brats**, remaining **chicken thighs**, anything left to grill
  - ⭐ **Dehydrated mashed potatoes** (just add boiling water), **green beans**

### Tuesday, July 7 — Departure
- **Breakfast — Pack-Up Morning:** cereal, bagels, yogurt, fruit, last of the milk;
  coffee. Eat down what's left so less goes home.

## Standing Snack & Drink Pantry

Available all week (everyone shares / restocks):

- **Snacks:** variety chips (baked Lays, Cheetos, Doritos, goldfish, Cheez-Its),
  applesauce pouches, string cheese, **nutella + bread**, trail mix, fruit
- **Fruit & veg:** watermelon, **honeycrisp apples**, bananas, grapes, baby carrots,
  celery, peas/green beans
- **Drinks:** water (lots), **2% milk**, **coffee**, Sprite, Dr Pepper, soda;
  **beer for the adults**

---

## Tips for Feeding the Group

- **Feed little kids a few minutes early** to head off hangry meltdowns.
- **Cook once, eat twice:** make extra at dinner for next-day lunches.
- **Host cooks, a different family cleans**—rotate so no one's stuck.
- Keep a **"kid plate" default** (plain version) at every dinner—plain pasta, plain meat,
  fruit—so picky eaters always have something.
