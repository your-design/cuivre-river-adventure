# St. Charles Historic District
{: .no_toc }

About 45 minutes south: a walkable riverfront, brick streets, history, and ice cream.
{: .fs-6 .fw-300 }

---

## Historic Main Street

St. Charles has a beautifully preserved **historic Main Street** along the Missouri
River—cobblestone-feel brick streets, shops, candy stores, and casual restaurants.
Easy, shaded, stroller-friendly walking; a nice low-key outing for mixed ages.

- **Lewis & Clark Boat House & Nature Center** — riverfront museum about the famous
  expedition that launched from here; replica boats kids can see up close.
- **First Missouri State Capitol** — the state's original capitol building, restored
  and tourable.
- **Frontier Park** — green space along the river, good for a picnic and a run-around.
- **The Foundry Art Centre** — art studios and exhibits if anyone wants a creative stop.

---

## Good with Kids

- Plenty of **ice cream and candy shops** to keep little ones motivated.
- Mostly **flat, walkable** with riverfront breezes.
- Short museum visits + park time = a balanced half-day.

---

## Tips

- Go in the **morning** to beat heat and afternoon storms; back to camp for dinner.
- Parking is easiest on weekday mornings.
- Check special **summer events** along the riverfront before you go.
