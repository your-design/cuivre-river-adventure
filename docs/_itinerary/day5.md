# Day 5 — Sunday, July 5 🦇
{: .no_toc }

The big day trip: Hannibal's Mark Twain Cave. Cool underground, packed lunch, dinner out.
{: .fs-6 .fw-300 }

---

## At a Glance

| Block | Plan |
|:------|:-----|
| Early morning | Grab-&-go breakfast; load up, hit the road (~1 hr) |
| Late morning | **Mark Twain Cave tour** (a constant 52°F!) |
| Midday | 📦 Packed/simple lunch in town; skip the hottest hours indoors |
| Afternoon | Twain sites / riverfront / gemstone sluicing for kids |
| Evening | 🍽️ **Dinner out**, then drive back to camp |

---

## Why This Is *The* Day Trip

Everyone flagged the cave as a **must**, so it gets a full, scheduled day. Bonus: a
guided cave tour is a **constant 52°F**—the perfect way to spend the hottest day of the
week *out of the heat* instead of melting at camp. See the
[Hannibal day-trip guide](/day_trips/hannibal) for details.

---

## Morning — Get Rolling Early

- **Grab-&-go breakfast** (cereal, bagels, yogurt, fruit; coffee to-go) so we beat the
  heat and the crowds. ([Meal schedule](/meals/meal-schedule).)
- **Caravan to Hannibal (~1 hr)**; the cave is on Route 79, ~2 miles south of downtown.
- The snack-pantry manager loads the **day cooler**: packed lunches, water, snacks
  ([Camp Jobs](/guides/camp-jobs#the-snack-pantry)).

## Late Morning — The Cave 🦇

- **Mark Twain Cave guided tour** — ~**60 minutes**, easy, lit, level walking; great for
  all ages (and strollers-of-energy little kids do fine).
- Bring a **light layer** (it's 52°F inside) and **closed-toe shoes**.
- **Book/confirm tour times ahead** in summer — call **(573) 231-1000**.

## Midday — Lunch & Stay Cool

- **📦 Packed, simple lunch** (PB&J + sandwiches, chips, fruit) at a shady picnic spot,
  or grab a bite in town. Either way, **keep it easy** and stay out of the 1–3 p.m. heat.
- Optional kid add-ons at the cave complex: **Cameron Cave** maze tour and **gemstone
  sluicing** (a big hit with the 6–12 crowd).

## Afternoon — Hannibal

- Stroll **historic downtown** and the **Mark Twain Boyhood Home**, riverfront, and shops.
- Keep an eye on energy and the sky; start winding toward dinner.

## Evening — Dinner Out 🍽️

- **Eat dinner out** in Hannibal or on the drive back—**no camp cooking tonight.** It's
  a well-earned night off for the cooks.
- Roll back to camp for a low-key campfire (or straight to bed for tired kids).

---

{: .note }
> **Heat-smart plan:** an early start + a 52°F cave + a restaurant dinner means we skip
> cooking *and* the worst of the heat. If anyone wants a slower day, a few folks can stay
> back at camp (beach, fishing, naps) while the rest do the cave.
